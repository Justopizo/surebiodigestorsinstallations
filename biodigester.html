Biodigester Information

How Does a Biodigester Work?

A biodigester works under 2 principles.

  • Biodegradation
  • Displacement

In displacement equal amount of water flashed is displaced to a soakage area and unlike in septic tank where you dig a soakpit that is vertically deeper in a biodigester we dig more horizontal this increases surface area for the water to soak faster.

In biodegradation waste is broken down to solidless water by anaerobic bacteria and to achieve this a catalyst (enzyme) is introduced.

How Many Users Can It Serve?

Any number of users, comes in different sizes as per projected users. Yes it serves institutions, hotels, residentials etc

Why a Biodigester and Not a Septic Tank/Pit Latrine?

  • minimal space requirements
  • no bad smell
  • no fill up hence no exhaustion
  • suitable for all soil types
  • cheap to install.
  • zero maintenance required.

Disadvantages of Biodigesters

  • purely excludes non degradable waste e.g. diapers & sanitary towels
  • purely relies on availability of water.

Advantage of Biodigester Over Septic Tank

  • After installation no more exhaustion will be required
  • No fill ups is ever experienced
  • Installation cost is more cheaper compared to septic tank
  • Occupie less space in your area
  • It's a life time human waste solution
  • Water from biodigester can be recycled to be used in garden
  • Environmental friendly since it has no smell
  • Much recommended by NEMA.
  • Favourable in all type of soil
